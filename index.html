Section 2: Character Narration (Whimsical, Storytelling Tone with a Baritone Twist)

(0:56 - 2:00)

Script:

And so, our heroes—if one could call them that—stood before the Tomb of the Infinite Chicken.

There was Gribble Shortstack, the halfling rogue… currently stuck in his own belt buckle.

Seraphina the ‘Magnificent’, whose last fireball singed off her own eyebrows.

There was The Silent Sister, whose vows forbade speech… which made her remarkably bad at explaining exactly why she just set fire to the tavern. (Were the monk able to speak, she would assure you it was the principle of the thing)

And Borin Ironbelly, the dwarf cleric… who, in fairness, did warn everyone about the cursed buffet. But no. ‘It’s probably fine,’ they said.

And let us not forget Lady Eveline de Vex—elven bard, alleged virtuoso, and confirmed disaster.

As the party’s designated ‘diplomat,’ she’s attempting to negotiate with the Tomb’s guardian… a stone golem who, and I quote, ‘hates jazz,’ and to whom Eveline was uncomfortably close with a ukelele.

But beyond the golem, armed with a sentient rolling pin and a deeply regrettable pact with a kobold named Stephen, they must face… The Clucking. Gods help them.
